Pantheon of Aaradia

The Nine Divine

Luceries

Order

Luceries, is the Divine of Order and chief deity of the pantheon. In the distant past he has led the divines and gods into battle against their adversaries. Above all else Luceries demands Order and its not so concerned with who is enforcing said order. This has led to many tyrants justifying oppression in the name of the chief Divine. Favored by nobles.

Amathera

Life

Amathera above all over gods has the most followers. She is also considered the Divine of fertility, many clerics making icons and totems to increase, or decrease the odds of procreating. Compassion, selflessness, and a sense of community are core tenets to be spread from sea to sea uniting all Amatheras followers.

Melia

Harvest

Melia is a favorite among the peasantry and farmers, finding most of her worshippers in rural towns and small communities. She teaches the importance of taking care of the land and creatures that inhabit it. Tear has many delicate ecosystems that can be disturbed and potentially destroyed if the people get to caught up in war or destruction of land to build homes.

Vitiate

Magic

Vitiate is the Divine of Magic. In the days before the Age of Collapse, many who worshipped the Divine had animosity for the Vitiate. Only dragons and monsters knew the secrets of the arcane. The only way for people to use magics was to have the power innately(sorcerers), make a pact with a greater power(warlocks), or pull power directly from the gods themselves(clerics) it wasn't until the Divines fought the Titans that wizards came about after dragons - creations of Vitiate - shared their knowledge with mortals with his blessing. Vitiates worshippers primarily consist of wizards, scholars, and clerics. While Vitiate advocates for protecting knowledge, he cares little about distribution of said knowledge.

Dromera

Hunt

Dromera, the Divine of the Hunt

Xethos

Storm

Xethos, the Divines of Storms, alongside his son Tychon, the God of Exploration are the chief deities among the Vissilian Empire. Farmers also pray to the god for rain. Xethos in this way represents a duality, great power to destroy as well as the potential to nurture. Because of this duality his influence is vast as many find different reasons to worship him.

Nuara

Death

Nuara, the Divine of Death. It is said that the only thing you are guarenteed in life is death; Nuara is inevitable. Nuara is represented by a vieled woman wielding a two headed scythe. At one end, a curved blade; at the other, the lantern of souls. The Al' Setseri worship Nuara as thier cheif diety, and follow a unque code called *The Way of the Ring* with Nuara at the core of the teachings. The greater moon is said to be the Divine herself and that crossing over during the night allows for easier passage into the afterife. With her soul-lantern she guides the departed to her shores so she may guide them into the afterlife.

Jalal

Deception

Jalal is the Divine of Deception, often called the Father of Lies. It is said the Jalal betrayed the Divines to the Titans causing the age of collapse. Jalal stands for more than just duplicity. Jalal exemplifies preparedness and practicality. He expects his followers to make rational decisions and always come out on top. He is a patron for many rogues and thieves.

Anelag

Chaos

Anelag is the Divine of Choas and is often depicted as the foil to Luceries. Despite being one of the Divine, Anelag is misunderstood. Many fear him as a force of destruction, but without chaos and change, people would stagnate and fail to progress. However, his power and force for change should not be underestimated, as his resolve is equally likely to mold you into a stronger being as it is to crush you under his indomitable will.

The Gods and Goddesses

Caustria

Affliction and Outcasts

Daughter of Xethos and Nuara. Caustria has few worshippers, but those that follow her are some of the most devout. Caustria is perhaps the most misunderstood amongst the gods. While many see her as the goddess of diseases and the exiled, her followers know her as a mother who took them in after rejection or exile. She is often depicted with snakes as her companions, furthering the general publics aversion to her. Caustria followers moslty consist of ostrasized persons, lizardfolk, kenku, corvum, and Yuan-Ti.

Tychon

Exploration and Adventure

Tychon is the son of Dromera and Xethos. He is often depicted as a sea god, and is beloved by the Vissilian Empire. Many of his followers make constant pilgrimages to shrines built in his honor that are scattered across the world. They can be found in the highest mountains, the darkest forests, underwater temples, and everywhere in between. Many of his most clerics are constantly traveling, only staying in towns or cities for a few days before heading out again, and have no true home.

Drailing

Revelry

Drailing is the son of Anelag and Melia. Drailing is the God of Revelry and is most often associated with parties and the consumption of alcohol; wine, in particular. Many of Drailings worshippers live in the golden hills of Kainlen, where much of the regions wine flows from. Often overlooked, Drailing's emphasis on community and generosity is another important aspect of his domain.

Kahl

Justice

Kahl is the son of Luceries and Dromera. The God of Justice is a harsh patron and his only stipulation is that's the criminal or criminals are brought are reprimanded at any cost. His clerics preach his "ends justify the means" attitude. Many see Kahl as a more brutal version of the God Lothar and use his name as a justification for enacting brutality.

Lothar

Vengeance

Lothar is the son of Jalal and Dromera. Those who slight others live in fear of Lothar. Lothar cares little for the severity of the slighting; only that whoever is carrying out the vengeance does so proportionate to the slighting. Vengeance is an act of passion which clan cloud a persons judgement when enacting retribution. It is a show of strong character that the slighted can temper their passion to achieve revenge without allowing that emotion to consume them. This is what separates Lothar from Kahl, the God is justice and Seluceris, the God of cruelty. Lothar has an "eye for an eye" outlook.

Atheon

Forge

Atheon is the son of Xethos and Amathera. The god of the Forge's most pious worshippers are those who exercise creativity by developing new technologies and sharing them with the world. Followers of Atheon strive to progress quality of life through hard work and ingenuity. Keeping your work secret as a means to garner power or wealth is frowned upon by Atheon.

Covelea

Hearth and Charity

Covelea is the daughter of Luceries and Amathera. Aside from her mother Amathera, Covelea is one of the most widely worshipped gods as she represents kindness and generosity for one's community. Many people keep icons to her in their home. Keeping a home orderly and tidy garners favor with the goddess.

Duesis

War

Duesis is the son of Luceries and Nuara. A patron to the various armies and generals that battle for dominion over Tear. During the age of collapse, Duesis led the armies of human and dwarves against the Titans, in a show of great unity. Now his people wage war against each other in his name.

Austere

Light

Austere is the son of Jalal and Amathera, and is the younger twin of Voltaris, God of Darkness. Austere demands that his followers bring light to the darkest corners of the world. His followers uncover hidden secrets and slay fell creature that call darkness their home. Those who follow Austere must be prepared to deal with whatever is uncovered in the quest for answers. Austere is the patron of clerics and scholars alike. Austere has a deep hatred for his twin Voltaris.

Voltaris

Dark

Voltaries is the son of Jalal and Amathera, and the older twin of Austere, God of Light. Darkness is the natural state of the world, it is best to be comfortable in it.

Aprocina

Art

Aprocina is the daughter of Xethos and Melia.

Triben

Commerce

Triben is the son of Luceries and Melia. Often associated with Tychon, the Gods of Adventure and Commerce go hand in hand. It is by Tychons will that new lands are discovered and by Tribens practicality that those lands are industrialized and profited off of. The Kainish are particularly fond of Triben, as the many guilds of Kainlen enjoy great wealth, and aim to maintain it.

Seluceris

Cruelty and Suffering

Seluceries is the son of Luceries and Dromera.

Kyltis

Luck

Kyltis is the son of Jalal and Melia.
